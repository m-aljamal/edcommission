# Education Conference 2025 Closing: Recommendations and Vision for the Future of Education in Syria

## Overview

![Conference Closing](https://res.cloudinary.com/dokawmydv/image/upload/v1764842832/531784057_1159578756204618_4052241139632520611_n_xwfxdp.jpg)

The conference titled *“The Reality and Future of Pre-University Education in Syria After the War”* concluded after two days of intensive dialogue sessions, bringing together researchers, educators, experts, and representatives of educational institutions.  
The conference focused on assessing current challenges and developing strategic solutions to rebuild the Syrian education system in line with post-war needs.

![Image 1](https://res.cloudinary.com/dokawmydv/image/upload/v1764842834/532154511_1159578442871316_2428158486600040329_n_k5y7as.jpg)

Conference activities included discussions on reconstruction, curriculum development, teacher training, technology integration in education, quality assurance systems, psychosocial support, and international partnerships.

# Key Recommendations and Outcomes

## 1) Reconstruction and Infrastructure
- Develop a national plan to rebuild and renovate schools ensuring safety and modernization.  
- Integrate safe learning spaces and technological facilities within new buildings.  
- Transform schools into stimulating educational environments connected to cultural activities.  
- Study the feasibility of eco-friendly school models.  
- Upgrade school buildings to accommodate students with disabilities.  

## 2) Administrative and Supervisory Reform
- Restructure school administrations based on transparency and efficiency.  
- Build a flexible supervisory body capable of leading educational transformation.  
- Adopt electronic management systems for planning and monitoring.  
- Organize training units for educational supervisors.  
- Introduce policies that enhance teachers' status and improve their economic and social conditions.  

## 3) Curriculum Development
- Comprehensive modernization to include critical thinking, creativity, digital skills, programming, and cybersecurity.  
- Integrate national values and life skills into curricula.  
- Design learning content aligned with post-war needs and labor market demands.  
- Apply the Romey Standard to ensure student engagement with content.  
- Modernize English curricula in accordance with global standards.  

## 4) Teacher Training and Development
- Establish national training centers in partnership with universities and international organizations.  
- Launch a national e-learning platform providing free training for teachers.  
- Develop a career incentive system connected to training and professional advancement.  
- Integrate psychosocial support within training programs.  
- Prepare educational facilitators in provinces to lead training modules.  
- Embed training into school schedules as an official responsibility.  
- Create a specialized diploma program for educational leaders in Syrian universities.  

## 5) Technology Integration in Education
- Develop national platforms for in-person and remote learning.  
- Provide devices and internet access to underserved schools.  
- Produce interactive digital content linked to curricula.  
- Promote self-learning and diversify assessment methods.  
- Build digital indicators to monitor students’ academic and behavioral development.  

## 6) Quality Assurance System
- Establish comprehensive national standards for education quality.  
- Create an independent body to evaluate educational performance.  
- Promote a culture of quality and self-assessment in schools.  
- Link school funding to achievement of quality indicators.  

## 7) Psychosocial Support
- Establish psychosocial support units within schools.  
- Increase the number of trained counselors.  
- Train teachers to work with students affected by war.  
- Integrate specialized emotional-social learning sessions.  

## 8) Local and International Partnerships
- Strengthen collaboration with UNESCO, UNICEF, and relevant ministries.  
- Launch experience exchange programs and joint projects.  
- Network with language, cultural, and communication institutes.  
- Apply innovative educational models in each province.  

## 9) Scientific Research and Innovation
- Support applied educational research and encourage innovation.  
- Establish school-based research centers.  
- Organize national competitions for innovative educational projects.  
- Provide specialized training missions for teachers.  

## 10) Sustainable Educational Policies
- Develop a national strategy for education in the post-war phase.  
- Allocate sufficient and transparent budgets.  
- Prioritize the most vulnerable groups.  
- Enhance early childhood education programs.  
- Support gifted students and those with special needs.  
- Promote flexibility and innovation across the educational system.  

---

# Expected Outcomes

These recommendations aim to build a modern and resilient educational system capable of:

- Rebuilding destroyed educational infrastructure.  
- Modernizing curricula to meet societal and future labor market needs.  
- Enhancing teacher qualifications.  
- Integrating technology as a core component of education.  
- Ensuring high-quality educational standards.  
- Encouraging scientific research and innovation in schools and universities.
